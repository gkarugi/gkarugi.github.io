About Us

About Us

Who we are

Centre for suicide research and intervention (CSRI) develops comprehensive and integrated approaches to reducing life loss and effects on the society from suicide and suicidal behaviors in the country and beyond.

We strive to promote and provide direction to efforts to modify the social infrastructure in ways that will affect attitudes about suicide in our educational sectors, social service, and health care systems.

As a Not for profit organization we focus on developing evidence-based community initiatives that will lead to a better outcome for individuals at risk of suicide, including their social support systems (friends and families) and the wider community.

By engaging experts in suicide prevention and other mental health professionals, our desire is to define and prioritize programs and models that will contribute to reduction in suicide rates. Our model is geared towards:

  1. Engaging in research that will contribute to knowledge and learning on suicide prevention
  2. Better predictions and detection to create safety nets for people at risk
  3. Delivering targeted postvention and interventions at the right time and at the right place.

The objectives of CSRI are driven by:

  1. The fact that suicide is the leading cause of death in our generation and many young lives
  2. The need to address the high rates of suicidal behaviors
  3. Lack of tangible research and systematic reviews on suicide in our country (Kenya) that would otherwise help in reducing death by suicide
  4. Lack of effective data surveillance system for suicidal behaviors that can play a role in monitoring the worrying trend for purposes of intervention
  5. Lack of the know-how on risk assessment and management of Suicidal behaviors that would otherwise have saved lives.
Our Objectives

Our Mission, Vision & Values

Mission

A catalyst for social change, to transform attitudes, policies, and services concerned with suicidal behavior through Research that reflect current knowledge and trends.

Vision

We envision a region where we have sustainable comprehensive evidence-based suicide Prevention & Intervention strategies as part of public health.

Values

  1. Compassion: We care.
  2. Integrity: We walk our talk.
  3. Diligence: We are relentless when seeking solutions.
  4. Humility: We strive to learn from our peers.

Our Goal

Reducing
the number of suicides.

To reduce the number of deaths by suicide by offering support to the individuals at risk, and promoting awareness on suicide prevention and post-vention.

Our Focus Areas

We desire to transform the experiences of those affected by suicide through:

  1. Research to improve understanding of suicide, post-vention, intervention and suicide prevention in our community.
  2. Teaching and training laypersons and professionals on how best to intervene and provide postvention services.
  3. Taking a leading role in policy and practice development in Kenya and beyond on how best to work with those affected by suicide.
  4. Collaborating nationally and internationally in partnership to jointly develop changes in the field of suicidology.

Our Postvention Goals.

  1. To help restore equilibrium and functioning to the family after a suicide incident.
  2. Promote healthy grieving for the loss of a loved one through suicide.
  3. Provide comfort to those who are distressed, minimize adverse personal outcomes (depression, PTSD, complicated grief), and reduce the risk of suicide imitation or contagion.
  4. Identify those most likely to need support. These are likely to include, but are not limited to, people who were psychologically close to the deceased (e.g., friends and family members), people who were already depressed and possibly suicidal themselves before the death, and those who might psychologically identify with the deceased as being similar in lifestyle, values, or life circumstances.

Our Results

How our results are
measured.

Our results will be achieved by redesigning suicide care systems and developing models of care to those affected in one way or another by suicidal behavior. Specifically: Suicide-Informed care as it reduces cases of suicide as individuals will be handled by people who understand the prevalence of triggers and its impact and also recognize the signs and symptoms of suicide ideation.

Creating an emotionally and physically safe space, and empowering the individuals with an active voice in collaborative decision-making; and respecting the person’s experience through active listening, being sensitive to the language used, being transparent, being trustworthy, and offering stability and consistency incorporating practices that promote a culture of safety, empowerment, and healing.
