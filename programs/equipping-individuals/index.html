Programs

Equipping Individuals

Through promoting life skills, social connectedness and encouraging help seeking and help giving behavior. This creates overall well-being, help them thrive, and protect their emotional health.

Activities in this program
Activity

Support group for people who those bereaved by suicide

This gives you the opportunity to meet others who have lost someone to suicide and share your experiences and feelings.

What does it involve?

Support groups run weekly for six consecutive weeks and last for 90 minutes with a maximum of eight participants. Groups are free of charge and are run by two trained volunteer facilitators from CSRI.

Benefits of attending a support group

  1. Understanding: People grieving because of suicide can feel very alone, suffering from a kind of loss they feel others may not understand. Even with supportive family and friends, participants find it helpful to talk with others who have experienced a similar loss.
  2. Safety: Participants tell us they feel safe in the group. They’re free to express themselves with others who are also grieving as a result of suicide.
  3. Coping: The group supports one another to come to conclusions that often lead to acceptance and increased hope for facing the future. Everyone who has taken part in a group has said they would recommend groups to others.
  4. Respect: Participants feel they are listened to in a respectful environment. Even when people are sharing things they may never have said out loud before, there is a non-judgemental and supportive atmosphere.
Activity

Support for people who self harm

The most important thing is to be there for someone, and to listen. Don’t be scared to make mistakes; the thing people normally need the most is emotional connection.

You may have spotted someone’s self-harm rather than them telling you about it. The person you’re talking to could be feeling really vulnerable, with feelings of guilt, shame and isolation.
