Our Programs

Programs

Strengthening schools

School community is a critical part for young people for as a support network and emotional safety net.

We work directly with high schools, colleges, and universities representing millions of students to put systems, programs, and policies in place to create a culture of caring that protects student mental health, builds life skills, and makes it more likely students will seek help and struggling students be recognized, connected to mental health care, and supported.

Programs

Strengthening workplaces

CSRI trains organizations to proactively address the early warning signs of suicide in the workplace.

Just as organizations have realized they can help reduce heart disease by encouraging exercise, they can also reduce suicide by promoting mental health and encouraging early identification and intervention.

Programs

Mobilizing communities

Our mental health is impacted by the world around us.

There are a range of factors that can influence the perspective, well-being, and behaviors of our people including teenagers, young adults, families, friends, media, and high-profile voices.

CSRI collaborates with these communities to create a culture of caring, grounded in deeper understanding and reduced shame and secrecy, and to collaborate on amplifying their positive impact while minimizing potential harm.

Inequality & suicide

People living in the most disadvantaged communities face the highest risk of dying by suicide.

  1. Financial instability and poverty can increase suicide risk. Suicide is a major inequality issue.
  2. Income and unmanageable debt, unemployment, poor housing conditions, and other socioeconomic factors all contribute to high suicide rates.
  3. Tackling inequality should be central to suicide prevention and support should be targeted to the poorest groups who are likely to need it most.
  4. There is need to create a more human and person-centered whole system approach. This includes a system that is more aware of mental health issues and the economic challenges people face.

Young people & suicide

Around 100 young people die by suicide every year. Our report investigates the relationship between loneliness and suicide in young people, and if or how they may be connected.

We spoke to young people across different parts of Kenya. Most of them told us that their loneliness played a role in causing their suicidal thoughts.

Worryingly, many of them said they didn’t feel able to ask for help with loneliness because of the surrounding stigma. And when people did ask for help, it often wasn’t available or suitable for their needs.

There is rarely one reason for a person to feel suicidal. Just as with suicide, loneliness is complex – it is a personal experience that's different for every person. Nevertheless, we found that, across the population, there is an association between suicide and loneliness.

What we are doing:

  1. There is need to listen to what young people are telling us: many of them are struggling with loneliness. Loneliness can be a serious health issue; preventing it could be an important part of suicide prevention.
  2. As an organization we have set out ambitious targets across to improve wellbeing in young people, including tackling loneliness.
  3. We have different campaigns to tackle stigma that young people are experiencing around loneliness.

Step Up Small grants program

We know that sometimes the moment of giving up or pushing forward to fight another day is the difference between an hour, day, week or month. We understand where you are and want to help you move forward to success. That is why we believe in helping when you need it most.

Job loss, poverty, and homelessness all play a part in Suicide Behaviour.

Do you identify with any of these needs? reach out.

  1. In between a new job?
  2. Needing short-term mental support
  3. Not knowing where your next meal is coming from this week?

We want to find a solution or help for you when you need it the mostWe want to find a solution or help for you when you need it the most

We provide the following:

  1. Small grants for individuals and families who need professional mental health support for 1-3 months
  2. We provide short-term help like meals, rent and medical aid.

Please note: We will support you with minimal grants, i.e. a bag of groceries, medical, and rent.
