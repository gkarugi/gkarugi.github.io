Meet our Team

The CSRI team are a passionate people who are actively assisting suicide victims and conducting research.

Mary Gitau
MA. Psych, MA. Suicidology, ACS
Founder and Executive Director

Mary’s focus is on extending the impact of CSRI’s mission and vision through research and designs its growth strategy. She leads the charge to effectively reduce stigma and risks associated with suicidal behaviours.

Samuel Gwaya
Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP), MSN, RN
Executive Advisor and Board Member

Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP), MSN, RN, State Inspector ND Investigator (Texas). Apart from advisory, Samuel acts as CSRI’s contact and coordinator in the United States of America.

Joseph Otieno
Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP), MSN, RN
Executive Advisor and Board Member


Joseph oversees the development and implementation of new training programs focused on suicide prevention, intervention and postvention, which includes community-based suicide prevention. 

Favor Khaoya
BA Finance & Marketing, CIFA K Certified
Communication and Public Engagement Lead

Favor manages and updates CSRI social media accounts to create presence and promote our work. She is the key person in all the CSRI communication, she sieves what goes out and to whom. 

Jacob Nderitu
M. Psych, Forensic Psychologist
Partnership and Networking Lead

Jacob is in charge of developing partnerships and partnership capacities, his role involves a focus on creating new opportunities for engagement with CSRI among its growing networks of diverse public and private sector stakeholders. 

Ruth Lumbasi
M. Psych, Community Psychologist
Community Outreach Engagement Lead

Ruth informs and facilitates the implementation and coordination of suicide prevention strategies to help ensure that all appropriate measures are taken to prevent suicide at the community level. 

Newton Koskei
BA. Psych, Clinical Psychologist
Counselling Service Coordinators

Newton is responsible for ensuring quality care to clients, survivors of attempts and their families and the community at large. Newton’s empathetic personality and his experience in counselling plays a vital role in CSRI’s impact. 

Chomba Njoka
Msc, Clinical Psychologist
Research lead

Chomba is responsible for providing strategic lead for the research activities meet laid targets in terms of research outputs. Chomba developes strategy which addresses a sustainable research environment & impact Agenda.
